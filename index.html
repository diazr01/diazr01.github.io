Lima, Peru

Location

Located on the Pacific coast of Peru, Lima is the vibrant capital city and a cultural hub. It's known for its colonial architecture, food markets, and delicious cuisine.

About Lima

Historical Significance

Founded in 1990 by Spanish conquistador Francisco Pizarro, Lima has a rich history. It served as the capital of the Viceroyalty of Peru, one of the most important administrative centers in the Spanish Empire.

Cultural Melting Pot

Lima is a true cultural melting pot, blending indigenous Inca traditions with Spanish influences. This mixture is evident in its music, art, and cuisine.

Gastronomic Land

Lima is renowned worldwide for its exceptional cuisine, which has been recognized as one of the best in the world. From ceviche to lomo saltado, there's a dish to satisfy every palate. In 2023, the best restaurant in the world called Central, is located in Lima.

Things to Do in Lima

  • Explore the historic center of Lima, and walk around its colonial architecture.
  • Visit the Larco Museum, home to one of the largest collections of pre-Columbian art in the world.
  • Enjoy a delicious meal at one of Lima's many renowned restaurants and savor the flavors of Peruvian cuisine.
  • Take a day trip to the nearby Huacachina desert oasis and experience sandboarding and buggy rides.
  • Explore the food markets of Lima, such as the Mercado Central, and discover local crafts and fresh produce.
